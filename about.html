C Fjord is built around a simple belief: AI succeeds when we design the work relationship—interaction, collaboration, and teaming—not just the model.

Foundation in Research

I am an assistant professor with a research background in human-AI interaction, collaboration, and teaming. That research gives me a practical lens for what breaks in real deployments: unclear roles, mismatched expectations, fragile handoffs, and uncalibrated trust.

Real-World Experience

I have worked across domains including manufacturing, software development, emergency response, education, and agriculture. The pattern is consistent: teams do not need “more AI”—they need clearer teamwork between humans and systems.

Through partnerships with industry and government, I translate research into practical frameworks: workflow mapping, role allocation, teaming protocols, and training that supports adoption without over-reliance.

My Passions and Goals

I care about outcomes that last: calibrated trust, measurable performance improvements, and teams that learn and adapt as the technology changes.

C Fjord exists to help organizations build those capabilities—so AI enhances teamwork instead of becoming another fragile tool.

The C Fjord Approach

C Fjord starts with work-as-done: people, decisions, and coordination. From there, we design interaction, collaboration, or teaming (whichever fits) and evaluate the system with human-centered metrics—not just technical accuracy.

If you want the full framework, start with the Approach page.
